The Practice

This is not content.
This is craft.

In a world drowning in generated images, Megabrixels is a deliberate return to presence. Every scene is constructed by hand — brick by brick. Every photograph is composed through glass that has a lineage. Every frame costs attention, patience, and care.

This is where fine art photography meets the world's most iconic building system. Where a 135mm Tele-Elmarit renders a Lego streetscape with the same depth and gravity it would give a real one. Where the line between miniature and monumental dissolves inside the viewfinder.

The work is the point. The making is where the meaning lives.

The Collection

Nine Worlds

Each section of the cabinet is its own universe — a curated environment with its own character, palette, and story waiting to be told through the lens.

01
Monuments
Eiffel Tower, Taj Mahal, Great Pyramid, Himeji Castle — the landmarks that define civilizations, rendered in brick.
02
Velocity
Ferrari F1, Red Bull RB18, classic Shelby Cobra, Ford Bronco — speed and engineering frozen in plastic.
03
Gotham to Manhattan
Empire State Building, Statue of Liberty, and the modular city street — urban density at miniature scale.
04
Vessels
Titanic, Mississippi steamboat, tall ship — the romance and engineering of the open water.
05
Refuge
The A-frame cabin with autumn trees and an Airstream — escape, solitude, and the good life.
06
The Street
Modular buildings, shop fronts, cobblestone texture — an entire neighborhood above the cabinet.
07
Cosmos
The globe, the celestial, the macro view — looking outward from the studio.
08
Noir
Black and white minifigure portraits. Character, shadow, and the Summilux wide open.
09
Failure Modes
Where Coherence meets Lego. Dioramas that visualize how systems break — and what it looks like to find your way through.
