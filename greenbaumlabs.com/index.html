Recognition

If this feels familiar.

When truth drifts from authority, you see it in the same places every time. The executive team says one thing in the earnings call; the frontline team lives something else entirely. Customer reviews tell a different story than press releases. People absorb the gap — and that absorption is invisible until it isn't.

When you wire a diagnostic lab into your workflows — local compute, adversarial validation, structured outputs — you stop guessing where the misalignment lives. You start measuring it. Not with surveys or sentiment dashboards, but with scored findings that survive challenge. The infrastructure is small. The implications aren't.

The Diagnostic Engine

Coherence

Organizations can't measure the gap between what they say and what the world sees. The disconnect compounds silently until it surfaces as a crisis.

A four-stage diagnostic pipeline — Collect, Extract, Score, Synthesize — that compares center narrative against edge reality. Every finding is challenged by an adversarial Skeptic before it enters the record. All local compute. No data leaves the network.

Seven instruments.

Tools that surface where organizational meaning breaks down, name the structural conditions causing it, and record what changes when someone acts.

FM ScannerWatches structural breakdowns. Records failure mode matches.
FFN SurfaceWatches early drift signals. Records field notes before failure.
System Nav LocatorWatches diagnostic position. Records where you stand.
Declaration LogWatches authority events. Records who decided and when.
Verified IndependenceWatches recovery claims. Records evidence that a fix is real.
Regression WatchWatches fix durability. Records whether corrections hold or decay.
